# Code of conduct

## Our commitment

We are committed to a professional, welcoming, and technically constructive community. Everyone
participating in this project's issues, pull requests, reviews, and other community spaces should
be treated with dignity and respect, regardless of identity, background, experience, or viewpoint.

## Expected behavior

- Discuss ideas and evidence without attacking people.
- Give and receive technical feedback respectfully and in good faith.
- Be clear about uncertainty, limitations, conflicts of interest, and AI-assisted contributions.
- Protect private information, credentials, vulnerability details, and other sensitive data.
- Respect project scope, maintainer decisions, and the time of other participants.
- Help repair misunderstandings and accept proportionate correction when harm occurs.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, threats, discrimination, stalking, or unwanted sexual attention.
- Insults, trolling, sustained disruption, or personal and political attacks.
- Publishing private or identifying information without explicit permission.
- Knowingly submitting malicious code, unsafe instructions, fabricated evidence, or undisclosed
  security exploits.
- Retaliation against someone who reports a concern in good faith.
- Any conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Scope

This policy applies in project-controlled spaces and when someone officially represents the
project. It does not govern unrelated private activity, but conduct outside project spaces may be
considered when it creates a credible safety risk for participants.

## Reporting and enforcement

Report conduct concerns privately to **bfvicco@gmail.com** with the subject
`[CONDUCT] engineering-loop-schemas`. Do not use a public issue. Reports will be handled as
confidentially as practical, shared only when needed to investigate or protect participants.

The maintainer may request a correction, remove content, temporarily restrict participation, or
permanently ban a participant. Responses will consider impact, context, repetition, willingness to
repair harm, and participant safety. Knowingly false or retaliatory reports may themselves violate
this policy. A person directly involved in a report should not make the enforcement decision when a
neutral reviewer is reasonably available.

## Attribution

and adapted to this project's current single-maintainer structure.
